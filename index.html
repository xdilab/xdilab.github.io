Research Projects

Our research lab is focused on developing cutting-edge technologies in the fields of Computer Vision (CV) and Natural Language Processing (NLP) for Computer Aided Diagnosis (CAD). Specifically, we are working on applied Deep Learning (DL) for localization and segmentation, sentiment analysis and response generation with the focus on explainability (XAI).

Knee Localization

Enabling more precise and efficient analysis of issues and injuries in medical imaging can be facilitated by localizing a region of interest within an image.

Joint Segmentation

With its high level of accuracy, segmentation can aid in identifying a region of interest for diagnoses and treatment planning purposes.

Few-Shot Learning

Few-shot learning provides the potential to significantly reduce the need for large amounts of annotated data, which is often costly and time-consuming to acquire.

Sentiment Analysis

The utilization of sentiment analysis techniques can assist in identifying expressed emotions and opinions, enabling the identification of potential threats or opportunities.

Question Answering

Question answering from Electronic Health Records (EHR) can automatically answer clinical questions by extracting relevant information and has the potential to improve clinical decision making by accessing critical information from the EHR.
